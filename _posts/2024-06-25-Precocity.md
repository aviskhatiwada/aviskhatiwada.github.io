Youth is beautiful. It's one of the few forces that keep me alive. Youth that is open, curious, orderly, and at work, is one of the greatest things. 
I met a prodigy who gave me some word of advice. Of focusing alone on something... of caring about something...

<i>
To answer your first question, I have never actually gone to school (which is a shame) and taught myself computer science, English, and mathematics, all without a teacher, just by reading books. The most important thing I want to tell you is that when it comes to learning something you love, the school or people around you don't really matter. I believe that focusing alone on what you love can sometimes lead to incredible achievements.

In studying computer science, I have always been helped by English books and communities. All the prominent books in this field are written in English, and there are so many amazing engineers in the English-speaking communities. TBH, As a native Japanese speaker, I envy students in Western countries who have easy access to this vast amount of information from birth.

I haven't been to America yet, but I am looking forward to visiting America and Europe in the future. I love Japan, but I think these countries are equally fascinating places. It's typical for Japanese guys, but even though we can read and write English, speaking it is a challenge, so I need to practice before I visit hehe.
</i>
